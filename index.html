Deep Learning for Complex Structural Variation in Cancer Genomes
Long-read WGS · somatic SV detection · cancer genomics
Summer 2025 May 2025 – Present ▾

Building a deep learning model to identify complex structural variants (SVs) in cancer genomes — the rearrangements, inversions, and copy-number events that drive tumor evolution but evade short-read callers. Working with long-read whole-genome sequencing data to train a model that can parse the ambiguous, multi-breakpoint signatures that define complex SVs. This project sits right at the intersection of my interests: sequence-level deep learning, clinical genomics, and the computational challenge of reading through noisy long reads.

CNV Identification in Helicoverpa zea
Bt pesticide resistance · 237 wild WGS samples
Active Feb 2024 – Present ▾

Analyzing 237 whole-genome sequenced wild H. zea samples to understand how copy number variation drives adaptive resistance to Bt pesticides — a major agricultural challenge. I'm building a CNV identification tool specifically designed for archived short-read data from non-model organisms, where reference genomes are messy and read depths are uneven. The project gave me a deep appreciation for how noisy real-world genomic data is, and what it takes to extract signal from it. Presented at PAG 2026, winning 2nd Prize in the CSSA Poster Competition.

Host–Pathogen Coevolution ODE Model
Temporal adaptation, allelic variation & seasonal forcing
Active Jan 2024 – Present ▾

Building a deterministic ODE system to model how hosts and pathogens coevolve over time — incorporating allelic variation, seasonal forcing, and birth pulses that make real-world infection dynamics so hard to predict. The model lets us ask "when should a pathogen strike?" and produces testable predictions for lab and field experiments. I was invited as the only undergraduate to present at the QEED Seminar, to an audience of 30+ graduate students and faculty.

Social Behavior Classification in Lake Malawi Cichlids
Few-shot deep learning · PI: Abhinav Shrivastava
Active Aug 2023 – Present ▾

Building a few-shot classification framework to identify the social behaviors of Lake Malawi Cichlids from video — a genuinely hard recognition problem because the fish look similar and behavior labels are expensive to annotate. I lead a team of 11 students and serve as the main point of contact between our team and PI Abhinav Shrivastava. It's taught me as much about research teamwork as it has about computer vision.
